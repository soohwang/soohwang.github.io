About Me

Soohyun (Ariel) Hwang (황수현) received her B.S. degree in Healt Management and Education with a double major in Social Initiative Track, Scranton Honors Program from Ewha Womans University in 2013. She received the MPH degree in Health Behavior and Health Education from University of Michigan in 2015. She is currently pursuing the Ph.D. degree in Health Policy and Management with a minor in Implementation Science at the University of North Carolina, Gillings School of Global Public Health under the supervision of Prof. Sarah Birken. Her main research interests are cancer survivorship and promoting smoke free policies. From May. 2015 to Mar. 2017, she was a research associate at the Veterans Affairs (VA) Center for Clinical Management Research and worked for optimizing veteran-centered Prostate Cancer Survivorship Care (P-CARE). Also, she was a research assistant at the Center for Managing Chronic Disease (CMCD) and an Angela Bowen Williamson Fellow at the Prevention Research Center for Healthy Neighborhood (PRCHN), Cleveland, OH.

Interests and Skills

I am interested in embedded systems, automotive security & privacy, vehicle motion tracking, and mobile applications for vehicle assistance. With safety and security being critical issues in embedded systems, especially with the advent of wirelessly connected and self-driven cars, I am very much interested in building new schemes that enhance their security and safety. As a result, I have built several security defense schemes on Arduino boards and have evaluated them on real vehicles and with real in-vehicle network (CAN) traffic data. Also, I enjoy developing new novel mechanisms in the forms of mobile applications or an off-the-shelf device, which can enhance the safety of the vehicle and thus the driver's.
